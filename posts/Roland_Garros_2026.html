Tennis 2026-07-03

Roland Garros 2026

There are recurring topics that we consider insignificant… And then there is our Roland Garros article. Yes, if you have been following us for a few years, you must have noticed all the references to Roland in our articles. While last year we declared our love for tennis mainly because we had the chance to see Jelena Ostapenko, this year we are going to repeat that declaration for very different reasons. 


If the story linking the creator of SBNN and Roland Garros dates back to 2014, the first time he set foot there was in 2016. This year therefore marked the 10-year anniversary of that encounter, 10 years and yet the names remain the same… well… we’ll see about that. 


A match between two athletes who returned to competition after becoming mothers, both experiencing incredible career resurgences. 
“Zuppo Beli” — a cheer that wasn’t enough (the fan probably meant “Super,” but his strong accent confused us). Despite playing beautiful tennis for over an hour, the Olympic champion couldn’t hold on. Bencic produced arguably the best tennis we saw all day: variation, precision, court coverage. But it cost her. Svitolina, known for her physical strength, stayed solid and struck at the right moment. More about fighting spirit than elegance, she turned the match around with the crowd behind her. 6–0 in the final set — a statement win. 


A great start to the day at Porte d’Auteuil. While waiting for the men’s matches, we wandered around the grounds. To our delight, a French-Czech duel in the junior girls’ competition caught our attention. 







Why not use sport as a tool for bringing people together rather than dividing them? 
Let’s celebrate our athletes — from Moïse Kouamé to Victor Wembanyama — and break social and cultural barriers. 
Long live sport!
